Weekly Mileage 2/7-2/12: 30
Saturday, February 12th, MIT Coed Invite, MIT
Distance: 3k Time: 11:26.35 Total Miles: 8.5
Comments:

My first collegiate victory...hot damn! Granted, it was a slow race without much competition, but seriously, today was exactly what I needed... I'm ready to roll and so excited for outdoor! Everything about today was great--I raced unattached in the old TP jersey which made me feel all goofy and relaxed, and then when I put on my spikes... oh man that just felt great!

Strategically, the race played out perfectly too. I shared the fastest seed time with another girl, so I knew I was in the mix, and without any time goals, I just figured I'd chill until the last three laps and see what I had. I relaxed into 4th early on, but by the end of the first mile I had moved into second without any effort. A lap later, I started moving into the lead, not really on purpose. I hesitated to make a move so early, but HBH yelled "If you're going to go, make it decisive!" and that did it. I decided that if I was going to pass her I had to make her feel it, so I pulled away a few more steps and just repeated that phrase in my head for the next few laps. With three to go I picked it up a bit more and finished with the win.
Extras: (20) wu, (35) cd

Comments:

the Z-center closed at noon today, along with the rest of MIT... for a snow day! Craziness! In any case, it meant that the pool was not an option, so I ellipticalled downstairs in the dorm's cardio room. Brought down the iPod and listened to Rancid while I did "weight loss 2" and "cross training 2" in succession. Somehow, it managed to be not boring.

Meanwhile, since I wasn't going anywhere, I tried to figure out how close ellipticalling was to running. According to the machine, I ran around 15-16k strides in that hour. Now, running, they say you get close to 200 strides/min, which is just under 16000 strides in an hour. So that's close. But the HR doesn't get nearly as high; I was around 130 for most of it. I can get it closer to running if I crank up the resitance, but then I can't do it for very long. So I dunno. No conclusions really, just thinking. Can wait to start running again. I'll be patient though.
