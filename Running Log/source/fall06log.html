Season Recap
    What a ride. Really, this was a thrilling season. MIT's first-ever national ranking, our best team finish ever at All-New Englands, a great showing a Pre-Nats and against some of the best teams in the region at Plansky and at our early-season tri meet. It was great to be on board a team with such a unified goal and where everyone was on track and trying to contribute to something greater than themselves. It's just unfortunate that it ended the way it did, with so many external factors limiting how well we could perform with the talent I know we had. In the grand scheme of things, though, that's how it happens. Every year, some teams succeed and some teams have bad luck. Five teams go to nationals and one or two others are just *that* close, and a few more falter even more. It happens every year to someone, I'm just sad that it happened to us this year, on the verge of such a turnaround. In some sense, this is just as much a good thing as a bad thing. This year, for the first time ever, going to Nationals was an achieveable team goal, not just a daydream, and now, teams of future years can have the confidence to make that happen. Next year's team, having felt how wonderful it was to be so close, will have the motivation and the talent needed to start developing a consistent, sucessful team for future years. Of course, we seniors won't have next year, or the years after that, but it's been great anyway. It was great to believe in ourselves so much, and to feel like our work was going to pay off in a bid to Nationals. That it didn't is of course a disappointment, but really just being in a position to make that as a goal was amazing. I had a heck of a lot of fun, with a few PR's to boot (6k under 24:00 and a Franklin Park PR). And when it all comes down to it, I'll have graduated with one NEWMAC championship, the highest team NE D3 finish thus far, the highest team All-NE finish thus far, the fastest team Franklin Park time thus far, a two-month long national ranking, and a bunch of amazing memories. I did what I could, both on and off the course. I never could have imagined how I'd feel at this point, at the end of cross country forever, and I'm still not even sure I know what I'm feeling. I'm feeling sad and happy, both, somehow. But it's been good.

Weekly Mileage 11/5-11/11: 32.5
Saturday, November 11th, NCAA D3 New England Regional , Springfield , MA
Distance: 6k Time: 24:44 Total Miles: 6
Comments:

Well I gave it all I had, but for whatever reason, the legs weren't there. Maybe it was the type of course; the short, steep hills always wear me out, or maybe we should have had a more conservative first mile, I don't know. All I know is that I can honestly say that I kept pushing it in my mind--there were moments of indecision and relaxation but every time I snapped out of it and kept pressing, but by the last half of the race I couldn't stop myself from slowing. I went out well with Adrienne for the first 2000 meters or so, coming through the mile in 6:00 and about 50th place, exactly where I wanted to be. After that... I don't know why things didn't continue along as planned. I finished only 3 seconds faster than last year, despite being in much better shape, which is a little frustrating (although maybe last year's course was faster? I was about 15 places higher). Anyway, I don't know. I gave it everything I had that day, and for whatever reason that wasn't my absolute best. I'm waxing poetic, here, but really, I don't know what else to do. I'm not upset about the race, because there's nothing that I can do from here, but I can't honestly say that I'm pleased with the way it went. I can wish, but on the most basic level, I guess I'm satisfied.

Weekly Mileage 10/22-10/28: 48
Saturday, October 28th, NEWMAC Championships, Holyoke , MA
Distance: 5k Time: 21:22 Total Miles: 7
Comments:

A dissapointing repeat of a 4th place finish, this year behind Coast Guard, Wellesley and Smith. The whole thing, really, came down to one mistake of letting the race get out across the field and having our whole team funneled back to about 40th place before the first turn, after which point there was nowhere to pass anyone until after the first mile or so. We (at least I) realized the mistake pretty much immediately, when we watched runners from teams that weren't even in contention flooding in in front of us, but even then it was practically too late to do anything.
Not to mention that the course was flooded with at least 2 inches of rain and/or 4 inches of mud, limiting the places where we could run. I don't know how everyone else felt, but I basically just had the sensation that I truly was pushing myself as hard as I could the whole time, but with no result; I just simply wasn't passing anyone. Maybe I took pizza relays too seriously, maybe I am getting a little sick or maybe today just wasn't going to be my day.
It's a really frustrating thing, to think that all of our talent couldn't come to fruitition on account of a silly strategical mistake--I don't know how we messed that up; if it was everyone's fault for not knowing, my fault for not passing on my knowledge of crowded starts from high school, or Coach Taylor's fault for not explicitly telling us that. I think, really, the responsibility lies a little bit in every body, not in a blame sense but just in the sense that everyone should be responsible for knowing and sharing always. Anyway, I am sad that Jessie and I didn't get to walk up and accept the trophy on behalf of the team and sad that this was the only opportunity and it's over. I know there's still regionals and the chance for 7 of us to represent MIT and prove how good we are, hopefully to the nation. But it's still too bad for everyone who finished their season today, and I feel badly that they don't get to feel like they raced in the race where we proved we were the best in our conference.

Weekly Mileage 10/1-10/7: 43
Saturday, October 7th, New England Championships, Franklin Park
Distance: 5k Time: 19:39 Total Miles: 7
Comments:

Long story short, we got an outside box and got pushed to nearly the back of the race coming across the field, which meant we had to fight pretty hard to get up to position by the first mile. From there, things pretty much went according to plan and I was working as hard as I could, coming through the mile in 5:57 and the two-mile in 12:33. After that, though, the extra effort at the start really took its toll, as I faded and struggled to maintain my composure. Even though cumulatively I passed people in every mile, there were definitely a lot of stretches (mostly the uphill ones) where 5, 6, 7 people passed me at a time. I got most of them back on the downhills, which were I think my strongest part of the race, but once it came down to the final 600 I was just getting sucked up and I was struggling to hang on. I mustered up one last burst in the final straightaway, passing two people and finishing with a just barely PR for the course (5 seconds). I think the amount of effort I put in, though was worth closer to 19:30, though, in a cleaner race.
More specifically, though, the real issue was that I was pushing hard and being very aggressive, but I was not specfic enough in my commitments. I made it through the second mile of the race by reminding myself that I was obligated to help out my group even when I didn't feel good, and that gave me the motivation enough to try to keep Adrienne in sight, planning for a moment when I would feel good enough to move up to her. That moment never made itself perfectly clear, though, and I spent a good chunk of the race just 5-10m behind her, finishing only 6 seconds back. I think next time, I need to say "ok, I will catch her by this point," and make it concrete. Not a bad day, though, but I've got more.
