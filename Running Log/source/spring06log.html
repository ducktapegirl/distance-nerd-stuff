Saturday, May 13th, All-New England Championships, MIT
Distance: 3k steeple Time: 11:37.40 Total Miles: 5
Comments:

Well the weather was bad, as they expected. A total of nearly 4" of rain for the day, temperatures around 50 degrees, although not as windy as expected. It really didn't get to me mentally, although it might have limited my warmup a bit. The times were kind of meaningless today, but I ended up second in my heat and I think I managed to get the competitive focus back. Taylor commented afterwards that he thinks it was the best race of the season, and I think I'd have to agree, at least from the perspective if the technical aspects of the barriers, and from a strategy standpoint.

The race went out with Easter from Colby jumping to the front, and I tucked in just behind two girls from Quinnipiac. The two of them spent the first mile trying to get in front/around me, just like I did last week, and I just tried my best to stay calm and just let the race play out, pulling ahead whenver a jump came off clean or whateer. It helped that my water jumps were all dead on; I gained a step on whoever I was with on every one. Just after the mile mark (we came through in 6:03), I decided to punch it. I remember thinking that if I sat and waited, I might never kick into the next gear, and it would just turn out like last week. By then a UNH girl was up in front with me, and we both went around Colby together, and traded leads for a bit. I tried my best to stay aggressive for the last three laps; I know there were times that I slowed a bit, but I think overall I stayed after it. At the end one of the Quinnipiac pair was up on me again, but I managed to hang on and just barely stay in front of her.

Well I can't pretend not to be disappointed, not so much with the time, but mostly with the fact that I dropped from my seed of 3rd to 6th, missing out on a shirt a second time and losing to three girls who I've never lost to before. The race pretty much went exactly as I imagined it, with top three at or under 11:20, except I wasn't in there. I can try to pick a few things that may have made it a bad race--a hard start (although I've started faster), the fighting in the first mile, a bad week of schoolwork and not enough sleep--but I don't really know. I'm not super-devasted or anything about the race, it was just kind of a bummer that I came up just short of top 3 again, and that I couldn't grab a few more points for the team, which stepped up big time. Saturday, April 29th, NEWMAC Championships, MIT
Distance: 3k steeple Time: 11:28.54 - PR Total Miles: 6
Comments:

I knew the race was going to be won with a fast time, but I was hoping it would be me doing it. I also knew that the freshman from Smith had only ever run one steeplechase before, and that your second one is usually significantly faster. I wasn't quite prepared, though, for the race to go out in 80 for the first quarter (even with two less barriers), and I was quite surprised to hear my first split. I had been trying to stay on the Smith girl up until that point, but when went by that split, I let her go and focused on my own pace and holding off the 3rd girl.
All in all, even though the race was a PR and a strong effort, I didn't feel like I was 100% mentally, and I even felt like my hurdling technique was pretty sloppy, even though teammates and other people who came to watch told me otherwise. I even smacked my right knee on one of the barriers during a middle lap; I dropped the knee on my trail leg. It hurt pretty badly, but not really until after the race, but I managed to stay on my feet. I think I was compensating a bit the rest of the race, though, as my left calf got extremely tired. My water jumps were some of the cleanest I've ever had, though, and I did eventually end up cutting down Smith's lead, but I think she's just pretty fast overall and didn't die as much as I might have expected from going out that fast. She ended up with an 11:12, so I don't know if I would have won today even with better focus (I never truly made a committed effort to catch her), but next year (or maybe next week?) will be a fun rematch. Anyway, I bought myself another race with today's effort, which qualified me for All-New England's.

Saturday, April 22nd, MIT Co-ed Invite, MIT
Distance: 5k Time: 18:40.80 - PR Total Miles: 7
Comments:

Coach thought I could run 18:40, but my goal was just to hit 90 second quarters all the way through for an 18:45. The seeds indicated that the race might be a little thin, but I tucked in behind a girl for the first three laps, which we went through around 4:20, so under pace. The next lap dropped to a 91 or so, though, so I took off from her and pushed the pace a bit. Coming through the mile, Coach told me that the Wheaton girl ahead of me (I was in third) was coming back, and I looked up at her and decided that I'd catch her in the next mile. So for the next four laps, I just kept assessing the gap and telling myself "I only have x amount of laps left to catch her," until I did get her just before the two mile, at which point I started chasing the girl in first.I knew there was a chance that I would lose focus or motivation or whatnot after I caught the first girl, so I really made an effort to maintain the focus that I had for the first two miles.
Admittedly, though, I didn't stay quite on top of things; I was looking down a little more and doing a little more "maintaining" than racing, but I held onto pace pretty decently. With 400 to go the girl I had passed earlier came up and started kicking, and I couldn't match it. I responded a half-second late, and once I got into my kick I gain a little bit back, but I think she had more finishing speed than me regardless. If I were to beat her, it would have to be by putting a bigger gap on her earlier. I put together a decent final 200 though, because the Stonehill girl I had been drafting off of earlier seemed to be right behind me and I didn't want to lose another place to her.

Saturday, April 15th, Maine Schools Invitational, Bates
Distance: 3k steeple, 800 Time: 11:35.64, 2:33.97 - PR Total Miles: 7
Comments:

Woo! What a day. A tie for a steeple PR, followed by my first 800m PR since...9th grade! I had been a little concerned all week since school's in full swing now and I've been tired a lot lately, but today was really a confidence booster. I had my choice between steeple and 5k today and opted for the steeple, hoping that at least one of the fast steeplers from Colby or Bates would be in it, but both already have NCAA qualifying times, so they didn't really need to, and as I sort of expected, they didn't. In any case, the same steepler from Colby who I raced over spring break was there, and unlike then, she was right on my shoulder today.
My plan, though, was to race through the first mile on pace, since that has been relatively managable the last two times, and then 200m later, coming around with 3 laps to go, I wanted to really dig in. So I went for it today, throwing in a surge with 1200m to go, and managed to gap Colby a bit. I had a feeling she wasn't expecting a move, so I really went for it, and managed to keep the lead through to the finish. Today was an near-excellent day technique-wise; I was really attacking the hurdles (teammates even said they noticed), and barely stuttered at all. I had one little slip on the first water jump, nearly two-footing it, but didn't go down and caught right back up on the next hurdle. Next week will probably sans barriers, but I'm really pumped about going after a NEWMAC win and an All-New England qualifier in a couple of weeks.
I don't have much to say about the 800, since it's not like I was in it to place anyway. I went out in the back of the race but moved up a place or two and came through in 74 (I think), trying to stay up on the front of my feet like I've been working on. around the 500m mark I picked it up and passed a few more people, but got passed back by some later. A 2:34 though, that's not bad for someone like me.

Just around then, the pack I was in broke up, with Marzouk going with a couple of girls up ahead. Taylor's instruction this time was to stay with the pack I was in (so that I wouldn't crash later, which was fine with me) although after a lap I moved to the lead of that pack and then surged a bit to keep them from following me.

My splits pretty much reflect that, but I kept telling myself that I didn't want to get passed (or last), and tried to stay in it. With two laps to go I really tried my best to pick it up a bit, but I was just hoping it would be over soon. I guess there was some semblance of a kick, looking at the splits. I missed D3 qualifying by 0.50 seconds, which is a bummer, but I think with this experience I can manage sub-19 next time I get in a good race. One thing I'm still having trouble with is getting really flat-footed when I get tired, hopefully that will eliminate my 96+ second laps. But I'm not upset with how the race went at all, and I'm glad I can finally record a PR again.
