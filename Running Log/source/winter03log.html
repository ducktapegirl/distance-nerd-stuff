Season Recap
    Rough. That's the first word that comes to my mind when I think of this season. I don't mean that in a self-pitying way; I don't even mean to say that it was too rough. It was just rough. There were so many things going on, it was getting dark, and cold, I was working a lot... it's not as though I felt like any one of those things was unmanagable but when I put it all together and slap on 45 miles a week I just realize that it has been an intense few months. Race after race after race... By the last two I had nothing more in me, mentally or physically. I'm on my fourth day off and I still don't even feel fresh. I don't want to just keep bitching about it because I know everyone else who was around for IAP went through the same thing, and I'm not saying that I had it any worse than anyone, but I have to admit, it all built up on me.
    So am I pleased with the season? I'd say it was decent. Recorded some solid times in the 5k and 3k, got that rookie record... but I wanted a little more. I wanted those two seconds in the 3k, I would have even liked the 6 to qualify for ECAC's (although I don't know if I would have lasted that long). I still want my mile and 800 times to be faster; I think they are not low enough yet to do what I want to do in the longer events. I know people say that the fact that I am getting faster as a freshman is good in itself, because of all that adjustment period stuff, but I don't think that applies, because I think that for the 3k on down, I am just finally running the times I should have run in high school. But it was good. I won't complain. But I won't be content either.

Comments:

Interestingly enough, I am not disappointed. I did horribly, I ran slowly, but I could not have run any faster that day. Weekly Mileage 2/8-2/14: 33.75
Saturday, February 14 - MIT Co-ed Invitational, @ MIT
Distance: 3000m Time: 11:09.45 - PR Total Miles: 6.5
Comments:

I don't know what to make of it. I feel like it was good, but not good enough. I don't even feel like it took a lot out of me. On the one hand, you can't complain about a PR, right? But on the other... it would be a lie to say I am not disappointed. Shooting for sub-11, and instead I'm two seconds away from a rookie record and 6 seconds from the qualifying time. The two seconds I could have had anywhere. Such as...the chunks in the middle where I slowed to 92 for the quarter, or the last 3 or so laps, where I just didn't feel like kicking or racing the girl that beat me, or maybe I could have had two seconds if I wasn't getting tailed and sucked off of again... I know that is something I have to learn to deal with and complaining is no good but... eh. I guess the rookie record isn't important; the question is could I have gotten the 11:03 to qualify for NE D3's? I think physically I was definitely capable. But there was something missing in my head, some unwillingness to push any harder. I don't know. I am just not risky enough in my races. That's why I do better in the longer events, I think, because I don't necessarily have to risk anything, I just run hard and hang on for as long as possible. But I need to learn to actually go faster. Well, at least now I'm sure I could hit the sub-12 3200 that I was after all last year.

Weekly Mileage 2/1-2/7: 36.75
Saturday, February 7 - Greater Boston Invite, @ Harvard
Distance: 5000m Time: 19:19.21 Total Miles: 6.25
Comments:

oh man, I did it. The goal was 19:29 for the qualifying mark and the rookie record, but I was SO scared I wouldn't be able to do it. I knew I was capable physically, but I just didn't know if it would happen. But it did, and man, I'm so relieved. Ok, so, the recap (once again, good race=long summary)...

   So I woke up this morning and was suddenly pumped, after a week of feeling pretty down. Deanna called last night, which was exciting, and I dunno I just suddenly felt good.
   Mentally, I decided to treat the race like an XC race; 6:10, 12:30 were the splits I wanted to hit. The only other thing I wanted to think about was not to forget that indoor tracks are smaller, and that even when someone is a straightaway up on you, it's really not that far.
   I came through 8 laps in 6:03, I believe, and then 12:20. A bit fast but it wasn't hurting so I kept at it. There was a little pack right behind me; I didn't know how many people but from the cheering I heard Jen, Nancy, and someone from another school. In the last mile the mystery third girl kept clipping me in the heel, not on purpose, obviously, but I figured that she was just sitting on my ass and wasn't going to pass me. It was annoying, but I can't blame her because I would have done the same had I been able. But I couldn't slow down to force her to pass, since I was going for a time. With 800 to go (counted my laps in 8's this time so I wouldn't mess up) the girl passed me and I was starting to feel pretty beat. I was slowing, hoping that I had given myself enough cushion to still qualify, when Jen came up on me with 2.5 laps to go.
   Man, am I glad she was there. Suddenly I remembered that I was racing, and picked it up a bit, and when I crossed the line I realized "dude! 2 laps is only 400 m, and in high school I always had something left for the last lap..." and just gunned it. With 150 to go I saw the Brandeis girl ahead of me (the clipper) and remembered my "only other" thought, and just started chasing. As I hit the turn I was accelerating pretty hard and I sort of knew I had her, then coming off the turn I went by and finished in front, 6th place. So stoked...

   And what made it even better were all of the other stellar races of the day...
· Jen "my goal was to break 20 but, oh look! I broke 19:30 and qualified for NE D3's instead" Gaugler
· Martha "well I'm always a stud and plus it's my birthday" Buckley (with the win and the NCAA provisional qualfier)
· Nancy "sub 20, mad PR, even though I almost didn't race" Benedetti
· Betsy "ha! I got you" Eames

Weekly Mileage 1/18-1/24: 42.5
Saturday, January 24 - vs Williams, RPI, Westfield,@ Williams
Distance: 3000m Time: 11:16.93 - PR Total Miles: 8.25
Comments:

oh man! first race in a while that felt good... which means this recap is going to be detailed ;-)

  It was a screwy-length track (180 yds, I believe) so I decided to take advantage of that and screw the splits and run for place; I figure I'm getting too obsessed with my times anyway. I was on the outside (of the 4 lane track), but got off to a good start, cleared the crowd and settled into 5th by lap 2, behind two Williams girls and two RPI girls. Somewhere in the first five laps one of the RPI girls dropped, one of the Williams girls moved up, and I tucked in right behind the other two, who sort of traded off the lead, sometimes going shoulder to shoulder.
  I was trying to count my laps; there were 16 2/3 and I had planned to try to pick it up when I had 8 to go. At that point though, I was dropping back from the two a few meters and decided that I would just try to stick with them. One or two laps later I lost count and couldn't understand the official so I just stayed with the tucking-in plan. HBH kept telling me to draft, which sounded all right with me, so I just hung out there. At some point HBH told me that if I was going to go, I had to do it then, and since I didn't really know how many laps I had left (it was 3 or 4), I figured, what the heck. So I started moving, and even though it was the beginning of a turn, I got around both girls pretty easily and didn't feel either of them come with me.
  In hindsight, I must have had 2.5 laps left, because when I went across the line the next time ahead of the two girls, I wasn't sure if I had one or two laps left. I thought the guy may have said 1 to me, so I started to go even harder (athough we had already picked it up), then I was thinking maybe I had another after this, so I stopped accelerating (but didn't slow down) and really just slammed it when I finally did have one to go. So basically, it was good. I responded to conditions instead of trying to control the whole thing, and I kicked earlier than I probably would have otherwise, but just like at the UMass Dartmouth race in XC, it worked out for the better, because I held off both girls for 2nd place and a PR.
