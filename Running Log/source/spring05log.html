Season Recap
    All in all, a successful few months. My goal for the season was an NCAA provisional mark in the steeple, which I was 14 seconds shy of, but in all honesty, that was quite a sizable chunk that I was trying to bite off. I was able to cut 27 seconds off of my steeplechase PR, though, and come within 5 seconds of my 5k PR, even though I ran it as a second event that day. I'm pleased with the gains I made in strength and in fitness and I think some of the changes I've gone through phyiscally are mildly permanent.
    Most of all, though, I learned a lot this season. ECAC's and All-New Englands were the two biggest track meets that I've ever qualified for individually, and even though I didn't perform particularly well at either of them, I now feel like I have the experience and confidence to look at them with a different mindset in the coming years. My success at Div 3's taught me that at the very least, I need to act like I know what I'm doing, and hopefully I won't have as many of those hesitations anymore. As a whole, this season has taught me to be a better racer.

Saturday, May 7, NE Division 3 Championships, Bates College, ME
Distance: 3ksteeple Time: 11:35.24 Total Miles: 5
Comments:

    What a CRAZY meet! Drove up the night before and woke up in Lewiston to rain and temps just over 40. Then the race was delayed over 45 minutes while they tried to fix the timing system. Finally we were called back out, by then we were all wet and hungry and my feet had started to get really cold. But everyone was in the same position.
    The race started and I settled next to a girl about 10 ft. back from Julia and USM. We can through the first lap really slowly (90), everyone must have been cold. The only other split I heard was the mile in 6:10, which surprised me in those conditions. I had resigned to race for place (3rd, hopefully) considering the weather, but when I came through that point and heard the time and saw Julia and USM still not more than 20ft ahead, I started to think maybe I'd run a good time. The next few laps were me and the Williams girl battling it out; kind of fun to have someone there the whole time. With 600 to go the two of us raced up to Julia and USM. I felt a lot of momentum right there, and just after the backstretch hurdle we both passed the two of them, and I took the lead. Technically, I was having an excellent day on the hurdles... the water jump was not so hot this week but I was probably subconciously dreading the cold.
    I led the race for just over a lap--had the gun shot for me, which is mildly rare--until with 120 to go, the three of them passed me back: Williams, USM, Julia, and I finished in 4th. Not good enough for a T-shirt, but I think it was one of the best races in my life. In retrospect, I had a definitive lack of confidence in leading the race. No one could ever say if I could have placed higher at all; there is always the chance that the girls who beat me were simply faster than me, but if I were to do it over, there are two things I would have done differently: 1) When I decided to take the lead, I had to take it. My memory of the race is a little fuzzy because I was so out of it, but I do remember sort of panicking for a second. If I'm in front, I have to act like I belong in front. 2) I should have said something to Julia when I went by. I mean, in the end, she passed me back, but getting passed is usually a discouraging thing and when you're running for qualifying times you can't afford to wait like that. I think saying something might have made her go faster which would have made me (and the race) go faster. On that note--I am not going to let my emotions or perceived "place" on the team affect my competitiveness. Obviously, I'm never going to "let" someone beat me, and Julia outkicked me fair and square, but I'm not going to say that I was not surprised or incredulous during the race. But that's not going to happen again. If I'm ahead of someone, I'm ahead of them. That goes back to (1).
