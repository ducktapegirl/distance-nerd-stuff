Season Recap
    Well, I started off expecting another disappointing season, but I can honestly say that at least it was satisfying (of course, from a social and team perspective, it was a blast). Nothing too exciting happened, Tufts was certainly the highlight with a 6k PR, but really I think I just got caught up in a pattern of trying to plan the race too much and never really being aggressive. Everything was too conservative and too influenced by a fear of blowing up again. I think I need to put myself into the race from the gun and trust on my fitness (which definitely improves every season) for the last mile. In any case, there was no doubt that school was getting to me a whole bunch this season, and I can't downplay that factor as much as I'd like to. I'm really looking forward to getting on the track where I feel like I'm in control and a bit more aggressive. It's likely going to be a 5k/3k season, and I'm hoping to bump the mileage up, since I haven't really done that since freshman year.
Saturday, November 12th, NCAA DIII New England Regionals, Springfield
Distance: 6k Time: 24:46 Total Miles: 7
Comments: There's never any point in dwelling on the last meet of the season, but I'll just jot down a few quick reminders to myself for next year. I felt better when I woke up this morning and decided to race. I think from that moment on, I should have focused on removing any doubts on account of having been sick, because those doubts stayed with me and caused me to race more conservatively, something that I had been doing too much of this season to begin with.
The start was really tight, 40-something teams with two people on the line. I got out what I thought was ok, hitting the mile in 6:10 or so, but in reality, it wasn't enough, because I spent almost the entirety of the race moving up, only to wind up in 115. Which means that if I went out 30 places further up, and maybe say I could pass 5 less people due to being tired, I'd have been much better off. But that's ok, that's just a lesson for larger, fast races that I forgot about this year. The same thing happened at NEWMACs. The positive side of the race was being able to work with Vicky and drag myself through the middle miles, I think it was a big push for both of us.
