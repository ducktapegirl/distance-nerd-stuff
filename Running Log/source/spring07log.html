Season Recap
    Hm, maybe I should make this a life recap, or at least a career recap. I don't think I'm ready to do that though, because I don't have all my thoughts sorted out. So I'll stick to the season review. I'd be lying if I said there was no disappointment in the season. It's true that team successes, all the fun we've had, pride we've gained, and memories we've created, will offset my own failures. (I don't think this is too strong of a word, I had goals and did not reach them. I'm not trying to be overly harsh, I'm just giving an honest assesment.) I am proud to be part of what is undoubtedly the best women's track and field team in the history of MIT. I'm proud to be a point scorer and a captain of this team, and I'm proud of my teammates who PRed and who met their goals.
    But at the same time, I've been chasing these same goals for years: NCAA provisional mark, NEWMAC win, Top 3 in D3, and to not even get close, to not even get outraced by someone better than me-- I defeated myself this season. I did not have the same mentality I did last season, and I knew it but I didn't know how to get it. Last year I started off with a win, a PR, a win, a win, a PR, and a PR, all in that order, all before the championship season. I felt like a badass. I felt like I could beat anyone seeded within 15 seconds of me. And then I took that and PRed at NEWMACs, qualifying for NEICAAA. I was on top of the world. And then this season I started out in a not-so-hot feeling 3rd, then broke my hand in the second race of my season, and my momentum was shot. My workouts were all great, all season long, which is the frustrating part. I still believe that I am faster than 11:28. 18:40 as a 5k PR I can live with, I think that was a pretty ideal race, but steepling under 11:20 is still something that I am capable of. Maybe I'll go after it again sometime, after a break (since I am going to be in another country next year). I mean, I really haven't taken any sort of break since 9th grade, no injuries or anything. And really, the timing of our championship season with regards to class isn't ideal, although that's no excuse. But yeah. I want to give it another shot. It frustrates me a lot that I really did wimp out so many races this season. I don't know what it was. I just... well yeah. I didn't do what I set out to do, and that's what happened. But I'll race again, for sure.

Weekly Mileage 4/22-4/28: 42.5
Saturday, April 28th, NEWMAC Championships, Coast Guard Academy, CT
Distance: 3k steeple, 5k Time: 11:38.31, 19:14.55 Total Miles: 8
Comments:

NEWMAC Champs, woooohoo! Damn, seriously, it's surreal... Coach kept telling us that "we look good," and apparently Coach Taylor was pretty confident on us winning too, but really, with Wheaton having won all of the last nine years that the conference existed... I just wasn't going to take anything for granted, so I volunteered myself for the double "if necessary." Coach took me up on it, also doubling a lot of people in the 1500/5k, so that we could fill up the 10k. All of it worked out well, as we went 1-2-3-5 in the 10k, and 1-3-4-8 in the 5k.
Steeple was first; I was going for the win. And really, in retrospect, I should have had it. The winning time was 11:29, just around my PR, and I'm so much faster this year. The plan was to sit and wait through 800m, since everyone always goes out too hard in a steeple, then get within striking distance by the mile. With 3 laps to go, I was gonna lay it down and try for the win. I made it through the plan through about the mile, at which point I just plain wussed out... argh. I dunno, I started catching Nora Youngs of Smith, and just sort of sat behind her as she slowed down (thus I slowed down too), until I finally managed to pass her with about a lap and half to go. As I started laying down my kick, though (or attempt at one), I started to see the leader in sight and at that point I realized I had so much left in me... if only I'd have gone a lap earlier! Argh! Yeah, I was kicking myself for a long time (still am).
The 5k was kind of a "just try to get a few points" thing, and I was significantly less nervous for it. Went out calmly in the back of the first pack, in about 9th. Came through the mile in 6:00. A couple of laps later though, Coach Brooks radioed Taylor and turns out we already had the meet wrapped up, and Coach yelled to me that I could drop out if I wanted, to save my legs. Haha... I think even he knew I wouldn't do that. Next lap, though, he told me to at least relax, which was ok with me, so I stopped trying to gain position and just hold my (barely) scoring place (I was 8th at that point). I think it would have taken a good chunk out of me to try to move up, although I think the next place (and possibly the next two) were within reach. Still came through with a pretty solid time though...

Weekly Mileage 4/8-4/14: 54.5
Saturday, April 14th, State of Maine Meet, MIT
Distance: 3k steeple Time: 11:52.13 Total Miles: 5
Comments:

Well, the dominating event of this race was certainly the fact that I managed to break my hand (5th metacarpal on the pinky of my left hand) falling over a barrier. Yup, that's right... the irony of hurting myself running is not lost on me. I was running a pretty solid race, came through the first mile a little fast (90sec pace) but that's where the race was going (I was trying to reel in the same Colby runner that beat me on spring break). I fell off a little after there, 95/96s for two laps, but I was still feeling decently strong and tried to give it a decent kick toward the end. Form overall was a little sloppy, though, and I ended up hurdling a lot of the barriers with my left leg forward. Coming up on the last lap, I was getting more tired and more sloppy but trying to pick it up at the same time. The combination of these, I suppose, led to disaster on the first barrier of the last lap, where I clipped a leg (I thought lead, Coach Brooks thought trail), and went down pretty hard... on my face. At that point it was instinct to jump up and try to get back into it, although I have to admit that the next four barriers were pretty much the least pleasant of my life. Anyway, I managed to finish without losing any places or anything, and faster than spring break. Based on my pace and the girl in front of me, I'm guessing I would have been at 11:35 or so, even with the poor hurdling. Don't know why I was so off today, the fitness was fine, I was just a little un-alert.
