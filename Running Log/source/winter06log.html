Saturday, February 10th, MIT Coed Invitational, MIT
Distance: mile Time: 5:36 Total Miles: 6
Comments:

This was really just a tune up meet, although a much more competitive field than ever before. Labuz and I were in the slow heat, although no one was seeded faster than 5:40. Labuz took the lead off the line and I settled into 3rd/4th. There was a good bit of jostling for position for the first 3 laps, I forgot what those shorter races were like. The pace felt slow at that point but I was wary about taking the lead and giving someone time to respond. At the 800 mark, though, I reverted back to my high school style of only caring about the 3rd quarter, and opened up a 5m gap on the field just as we crossed the line. We were a bit off of the target pace for a 5:30, which was what I was shooting for, but tried to push it. My legs were feeling quite unresponsive and I was just hoping that no one would catch back up. Coming around the last 150 I felt someone catching back up to me and put in some semblance of a kick to just barely hang on for the heat win.

Tuesday, January 16th
Workout: intervals Minutes: - Miles: 8.5
Comments:

Coach has us on the track this week since we weren't performing quite up to his expectations, which I was ok with today, considering the 20-degree weather. I know I'm getting spoiled with this January, but still, I wasn't in the mood for a fartlek outside. Plus, I wanted to be on the track to help get my focus back under control and to test if I really was in shape or not. We had a brutal 4x800 (2:00 rest), followed by 5:00, then 8x400. Karen was late and with the ability spread on the team we ended up with 4 people running the same workout by themselves. My goal times were 2:55, then "a pace a couple seconds faster" for the 4's. I had no idea how this would go, based on my last few races, but I decided to just suck it up and give it a shot. Amazingly, enough, things turned out ok. I don't want to oversell the workout, but it was certainly better than any of my races so far and somewhat comprable to an early season outdoor workout last year.

We did the workout at BU, to take advantage of their nice wide turns and banked track. I basically spent most of the workout telling myself to keep not only my eyes but my whole head and body looking up, trying to basically "get tall" and keep good form, which I think helped a lot. The 800's went by with relatively little strain, (key word: relatively), and I spent the 5:00 rest wondering why I couldn't do that a few days ago. The 400's were a little rougher by the time I got to the 3rd one, and #4 fell a part slightly. On the 5th one I tried to get back in it, comparing it to the 3rd quarter of a race, but it was the 6th one that really came around. I don't know what it was, I was just thining about the form thing, and suddenly I was moving and Coach Taylor even commented on it as I came around the first lap. After coming through in 84 for that one, I didn't want to slow down and really put my mind to bringing in the last two well. On the very last one, Coach had Lauren wait for me, and I got a little antsy at the start with someone else to run with, but kept it together to still finish well.
