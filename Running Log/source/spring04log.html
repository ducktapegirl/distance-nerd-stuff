Season Recap
    So I took a little time to write this because I was sort of in denial that the whole thing was over. I've already explained my disappointment with not having another chance to steeple. It's basically the opposite of an injury; instead of having races left and not being able to run, I'm able to run but have no races left. And the whole "three years left" thing isn't really consolation, because racing is about being able to put it together at the exact moment that it counts, and I'll have the same responsbility three more times in coming years.
    But after a few days of thinking and some lovely conversations with old teammates (really just Julie), I think it's all right. Julie made me realize that I really have done a lot, this season and this whole year. I guess I let ambition cloud my perspective a bit. I mean, it was sort of in my head that last year in high school I should have been running these certain times, and so when I finally PRed and hit them this year, I pretty much took it for granted. But I really shouldn't. I mean, I ran some solid times in the shorter races, and then I picked up a whole new freakin event. That's pretty significant, I suppose, and even though I didn't get to do everything I wanted to, it's ok I guess. Next year...
    I was reading some of my old stuff from camp and I read this one story about an Olympian who had a disappointing race at the Games, and he had to wait four years to get his shot again. I'm lucky in that regard, I suppose, because I only have to wait one year and I've got all these other things in between to distract me. And plus, once I get to next year, I can start off not knowing what I can do, so I won't be mentally limiting myself. So I think it'll be all good. Gearing up now for a nice 550 mile summer.

4/25-5/1 Weekly Mileage: 24
Saturday, May 1- New England Division III's, Springfield
Distance: 3k steeple Time: 12:28.63 Total Miles: 3.5
Comments:

    Damn, that was frustrating. My fever came back last night, and even though it was gone completely by the time I went to bed, and never came back today, I was still left with nothing. My muscles were quivering a little in the morning, but I didn't want to psyche myself out (I was already losing confidence) so I just played it off. I figured it was my last race if I didn't run, and possibly not my last race if I did, so why not? Well... it ended up being my last race.
    I don't know, there's not much use discussing the race itself because it was not meant to be a legitimate shot. I went out fine, even a few seconds under the plan for the mile (88/2:57/6:10 or thereabouts). Right after the mile though, right after I came through the finish and saw the 3 laps to go sign, I just turned to jello. I wanted to drop the race then and there, but I figured I'd just go around to the 200 and ask HBH if I could. Of course I didn't ask, so I just kept running, but every time I came up to a barrier I literally thought I was going to cry. I ended up nicking my knee on one of the barriers actually, that's how lazy I got, and apparently I was swerving on the straights, which I don't remember at all... yeah basically I just slogged it in with little energy or heart. I finished the race and really did break down and start crying; I think that's the first time in my life that's ever happened. Really sucks that I never got a really good shot at this event. 11:55 was totally within reach, and it sucks that all this work and all the fitness that I gained is going to shit, and that I have no more chances until next year. Definitely NOT ready for this season to be over after 5 races.

4/18-4/24 Weekly Mileage: 34.5 +XT
Saturday, April 24- NEWMAC Championships, Springfield
Distance: 3k steeple/1500m Time: 12:02.03 (PR)
5:22.38		 Total Miles: 7
Comments:

Well there was no wimping out today, which has been a trend through some of my races. It was crazy windy down the whole back 200 all day; times were slow across the board. Even though I didn't get the ECAC mark today, I'm pretty sure that my race was worth around 11:55 in good conditions, so hopefully next week I'll just lay it out. I'm still looking down at the barriers too much, but I think my hurdling is getting better. Ran a solid race for 5th place, matching my seed, which was fine since there really wasn't anyone within 20 seconds of me either way.
     The 1500 was 50 minutes later, which was a bit harsh. I was seeded 8th in the fast heat, but Julia had a NT and was in the slow heat, so I was trying to bust out 6th or 7th. With the wind I really was only running for place, plus it was a team scoring meet, so I tried to tuck in with the pack and just maintain. Worked out for the first lap, came through in 80, but then I really started feeling it. Everything hurt, two or three girls went around me, and I tried my best to hang with them. I knew I wasn't in last, by 2 laps to go I wasn't sure what I had left. I'm really happy though, because at that point I usually just start "coasting," and sort of resign myself to whatever is going to happen. But this time I actually kept my heart in it and still tried for places. On the final 300 there was a Smith girl and a Coast Guard girl about 75m up; HBH was yelling "they're coming back to you," and when I looked up, it definitely looked like the Smith girl was. The USCGA girl started pulling away and I just sort of mentally started following her; by 100 to go I was just barely within kicking reach of the Smith girl. At probably the last possible second, I found one more gear. Smith was teetering out into lane 2 and I figured that one a straightaway it wasn't rude to pass on the inside, so I just stared at the finish line and shot up lane 1. 5:22.38 to 5:22.71, good for 7th in the heat. Unfortunately, Julia's heat actually had a second person who should have been seeded higher, and I wound up 9th, just one place out of scoring. But I can't control that.
