Weekly Mileage 1/22-1/28: 26+XT
Saturday, January 28th, Bowdoin
Distance: 5k Time: NT Total Miles: 6
Comments:

What a crazy day. I was pretty keyed up and feeling good about today, and was in the same mood when I started the race. The IT band hurt a little bit at the tail end of the warmup, but felt ok during strides. It hurt again at the beginning of the race, but went away within 3 or 4 laps. I settled into a good pace early, and after a Colby girl passed me about 1000m in, I was able to tuck in right behind her and just not think for a while, while she pulled me along at pretty consisent 47's (hey, I hate when people do it to me, but she made the move). Just over a mile into it, Coach convinced me that I could catch Lauren, so I picked it up a bit, passing Colby and throwing in a surge or two over the course of a lap in order to shake her. And then...

The fire alarm went off.

They tried to keep the race going while evacuating the rest of the field house, but just over two miles into the race, we were pulled off the track. Long story short, the race ended then and there, they gave us points based on the places we were in at that instant, and of course, no times. It was as fair as they could do and I was given 4th place.

There are a lot of woulda/couldas and what if's that I could play out from today. I felt really good today; we were closing in on the Tufts girl and maybe the places would have changed, and I was on pace for something in the 19:40's, maybe even a D3 qualifier. But hey, maybe I would have crashed or aggravated my ITB in that last mile. All in all, what happens happens, and if a squirrel chews through the wires in a transformer and starts an electrical file, that's the way the cookie crumbles. I had no more events after the 5k, and when the meet resumed 2 hours later, I had a good time cheering for everyone and watching one of the most incredible team performances I've seen in a long time. There were PR's and big pull-throughs all around, and it was a good meet.

Saturday, December 10th, Intrasquad Meet, MIT
Distance: 3k Time: 11:16.85 Total Miles: 6
Comments:

Wow, surprised myself on that one. I was optimisically hoping for 11:20-11:25, since that's what I ran in that February meet last year (since I took indoor off). We warmed up a little early (about an hour ahead) so I did another 400 about 20 min. before the race, picking it up on the last 200. I had entertained the idea of just trying to stick with Finn from the gun, since it wouldn't have mattered too much if I dropped back, but when we went out, I could tell she was way out of my reach and that would have been stupid, so I dropped back. Hit 44 for the first lap, then pretty much stayed at 90 second quarters from there on.
It was a great race, style-wise, if you want to call it that. Looking back, I was a well-paced race that exhibited a lot of focus on my part. I felt really strong and in control, too.There were also a couple decisions that, had I not made them, probably would have had me end up a good 20 seconds back. One was just after the third lap, Christa was still just behind me, and as I hit the fourth lap I decided to make a move, to avoid getting drafted. That worked out well, keeping me on 6:00 pace and opening up a gap. Next, coming through the mile, I told myself that I need to throw in another surge, both to make sure I had opened the gap and also since it takes an increased effort to run even splits. Again, a lot of times this is something I'd be afraid to do, but it worked out well, kept me on pace and didn't tire me out. A well timed kick with 600 to go and a strong last lap (40) brought me in under 11:20. Four seconds faster than the last time I ran on Dec. 10, freshman year, and a month ahead of the last time I hit 11:16.
