Season Recap
    One heck of a season, what can I say? It's funny how I think back to Torrey Pines and I think of Brenna and Taylor and others and how I told myself, "If I can break 20 (for the 5k), I'll consider myself 'fast'." And now, here I am, not going over 20 since the second race of the season, and all I can think now is "I want to run faster." I guess that's the nature of all runners though. Seriously, though, I don't know how this all happened. I mean, racing 5k's every week instead of twice a season kind of increases my chances of doing well at that distance, but... I guess for one thing I really put in the work this summer, it's kind of scary to think that I'll do that much (most likely more) all over again next summer, but I know by the time that rolls around I'll be pumped for it again. Right now I'm a bit tired, it's crazy to think that in HS I'd be going for another two weeks; I don't think I could take it at this point. I also need new shoes.
    Well analysis is only educational if I am specific about things, so here goes: I think one of the biggest thing that helped my season this year was that I was way more aggressive. In HS I think I got stuck in a rut where I was thinking "oh, I'm this number runner and so and so is faster than me," etc etc. When I showed up here though, I have to admit, I was a bit cocky at first, thinking like, oh man, our team last year could probably take this team in dual meet and crap like that. But I think that little edge that I had actually helped out--it goes with my theory that every good athlete needs to have some hint of an "I'm better than you" attitude toward their competition. I had nothing to lose so I wasn't afraid to take risks. There was no place on the team where I had already decided I should be, so I just wanted to see what I could do. Kind of funny actually, on my original goals sheet for this season I wrote that I wanted to see if I could make top 7 and that I hoped I could break 20...haha. Anyway, so yeah, basically, I think the biggest thing I would say I did all season was learn how to race. I was starting to figure it out finally last year in track, in the 3200, where I learned to control my first 800 and rely on my strength to move myself up later on. This year, I took that style and carried it further, and on top of that got a hell of a lot stronger mentally, learning to suck it up when things got tough in a race (good memory of that... Engineer's Cup).
    Of course, not everything can be perfect. Despite racing better than ever before, I feel like I continued my habit of having a better beginning season than end. Not that I did poorly at the end, clearly if the NEWMAC's course was measured right I definitely peaked well. For some reason though, I just felt--stronger--in my earlier races. Like I had a little more control, I found a few more extra gears at the end... of course having the power to accelerate so much at the end might mean that the rest of my race was not a good. Truth be told, I can't tell if my last few races were good or not. I don't get much feedback from the coaches in that respect, but whatever, not that I really ever got that feedback from Coach Thorne. I don't know, I supposed if I asked HBH he'd give me some thoughts. I really don't know. I mean, I felt like I raced well in my last race, but the time really doesn't reflect it, and I don't know if the place does, and I just can't figure it out. So maybe I didn't fall apart at the end. For some reason, I just kind of feel like my best races were earlier on, UMass Dartmouth and all NE's. But maybe not. All in all though, I'm pleased with myself. Now... two weeks off and then...track season!

Don't like to overanalyze the last meet of the season but...I'll comment briefly:
